\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper introduced a Bi-level Adversarial Reinforcement Learning (Bi-ARL) framework for network threat detection. By modeling the attacker-defender interaction as a nested leader-follower game, the method goes beyond static supervised learning and standard simultaneous-update RL. In our reproduced NSL-KDD experiments, Bi-ARL achieves the best mean F1 score among the RL baselines and substantially lowers FPR compared with vanilla PPO, which confirms that adversarially informed policy optimization can improve the clean-data detection trade-off in a controlled setting. However, the broader evaluation across UNSW-NB15 and CIC-IDS2017 leads to a more restrained conclusion: strong supervised tabular baselines remain clearly stronger and more stable than the current RL formulation, and the relative ranking is highly sensitive to the evaluation protocol. The current system should therefore be viewed as a research prototype that demonstrates a useful RL perspective, rather than as a submission-ready robust IDS. The most important next steps are to stabilize the inner-loop optimization, reduce seed sensitivity, strengthen modern baseline coverage, and extend the evaluation to harder real-world protocols and additional public datasets such as CSE-CIC-IDS2018.

\title{Bi-level Adversarial Reinforcement Learning for Robust Network Threat Detection}

\author{Jun Zhu
    \thanks{J. Zhu is with the School of Cyber Science and Engineering, Nanjing University of Science and Technology, Nanjing, China.}
}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
    Network intrusion detection systems (IDS) face a fundamental challenge: static detectors trained on fixed datasets degrade when confronted with adaptive attackers who optimize evasion strategies in response to the deployed model. This paper proposes \textbf{Bi-level Adversarial Reinforcement Learning (Bi-ARL)}, a framework that formalizes attacker-defender interaction as a Stackelberg game and trains the detector against an adversary that is repeatedly adapted toward its best response, rather than against a static or simultaneously updated opponent. We further transfer the same bilevel adversarial training principle to stronger supervised tabular detectors, yielding \textbf{BiAT-MLP} and \textbf{BiAT-FTTransformer}. Systematic evaluation across NSL-KDD, UNSW-NB15, and CIC-IDS2017 demonstrates three consistent findings: (1) on NSL-KDD, Bi-ARL achieves the best mean F1 among RL baselines (80.17\%) and reduces FPR by 29.16 percentage points versus vanilla PPO; (2) on UNSW-NB15, BiAT-FTTransformer reaches 89.51\% mean F1 with 14.97\% mean FPR, attaining the lowest false positive rate among all neural detectors; and (3) both FGSM and PGD robustness analysis reveals that bilevel training provides partial adversarial robustness on NSL-KDD, motivating further work on inner-loop stability. These results establish that explicit leader-follower bilevel optimization is a principled and effective approach for adversarially robust IDS training, with clear pathways for improvement on modern benchmarks.
\end{abstract}
